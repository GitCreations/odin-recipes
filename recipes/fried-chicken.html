Chicken even the Colonel would be proud of.

Description

This fried chicken features an incredibly thick and craggy crust that is heavily seasoned with a blend of eleven secret herbs and spices inspired by KFC. To ensure the meat stays juicy and tender all the way through, the chicken is soaked in a Southern buttermilk marinade. The recipe also utilizes a specific breading trick to create an ultra crunchy and nubbly exterior.

For the ultimate homemade fast food experience, serve this chicken alongside French fries and mashed potatoes with gravy.

All the ingredients you need for that authentic KFC style fried chicken!

  • Chicken: 1.5 kg (about 3 lbs) of bone-in, skin-on pieces (thighs, drumsticks, and wings work best).
  • Buttermilk: 500ml (2 cups).
  • Salt: 1 tablespoon.
  • Egg: 1 large egg (whisked into the marinade to help the flour stick).

The Breading (KFC Copycat Mix)

  • Base: 2 cups all-purpose flour and 1/2 cup cornstarch (cornstarch is the secret to extra crunch).
  • 2/3 tablespoon of salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 tablespoon of dried basil
  • 1/3 tablespoon of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of celery salt
  • 1 tablespoon of ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of dried mustard
  • 4 tablespoons of paprika
  • 2 tablespoons of garlic salt
  • 1 tablespoon of ground ginger
  • 3 tablespoons pf ground white pepper

The method.

Begin by whisking the buttermilk, egg, and salt together in a large bowl. Submerge your chicken pieces in the mixture, cover, and refrigerate for at least four hours, though overnight is ideal for the best results. This step tenderizes the meat and ensures it stays juicy during the high-heat frying process.

In a large shallow dish, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, and all eleven herbs and spices until the color is uniform. To achieve the secret nubbly texture, drizzle two to three tablespoons of the buttermilk marinade into the dry flour mix. Use your fingers to rub the liquid into the flour until small clumps form throughout the breading, as these clumps create the craggy and ultra-crunchy crust.

Remove a piece of chicken from the marinade and let the excess liquid drip off before placing it into the flour mixture. Press down firmly so the flour and the small clumps adhere to the surface, ensuring every nook and cranny is heavily coated. Place the breaded chicken on a wire rack and let it rest for about fifteen minutes to help the coating set so it does not fall off in the oil.

Heat a large pot with about three inches of neutral oil to 175°C. Carefully lower the chicken into the oil in batches to avoid overcrowding and fry for twelve to fifteen minutes while turning occasionally. Once the crust is deep golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 74°C, remove the pieces and let them rest on a clean wire rack for five minutes before serving to keep the bottom from getting soggy.

Easy Creamy Gnocchi Sauce Recipe.

Description

Certain to be a family favorite, this creamy gnocchi sauce recipe is a fusion of rich mascarpone, fragrant shallot, and robust garlic cloves, simmered together with heavy cream, cheese, and pillowy gnocchi for a quick and easy meal.

What the hell is Gnocchi?

Gnocchi are a type of Italian dumpling most commonly made from boiled potatoes, flour, and eggs. The mixture is rolled into long ropes, cut into bite-sized nuggets, and often pressed against a fork or a wooden board to create ridges that catch the sauce. Once boiled, they develop a soft, "pillowy" texture that is denser than pasta but lighter than a traditional bread dumpling. While potato is the standard base, they are also frequently made with ricotta cheese (known as gnudi) or semolina flour.

Now for the fun part... the ingredients!

  • Butter (or olive oil)
  • Garlic (fresh cloves or powder)
  • Shallot (posh onion)
  • Heavy cream
  • Mascarpone
  • Shredded Gruyere
  • Grated Parmesan
  • Salt (preferably sea salt)
  • Black pepper
  • Gnocchi (homemade or store-bought)

How to make creamy Gnocchi sauce?

Start by boiling a large pot of salted water. While that heats up, melt some butter in a big skillet over medium heat and sauté your diced shallots for about three minutes. Once they’re soft, stir in the garlic for a minute until it smells great—just be careful not to let the butter or garlic burn, as the flavor turns bitter quickly.

Next, slowly whisk the heavy cream into the skillet, then stir in the mascarpone until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season it with a bit of salt and pepper, turn the heat down to low, and let it simmer for a few minutes. Take the pan off the heat and whisk in your Gruyère and Parmesan until the cheese is completely melted. Give it a quick taste to see if it needs more salt or pepper.

Finally, drop the gnocchi into your boiling water. As soon as they float to the top, use a slotted spoon to move them straight from the water into the sauce. Give everything a good toss to coat the gnocchi evenly, then serve it right away with an extra sprinkle of Parmesan and black pepper on top. Enjoy!
